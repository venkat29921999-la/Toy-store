Our Process

From forest floor
to playroom floor

Six stages, each one done by hand before the next begins.

01

Sourcing

FSC-certified maple and beech, logged from replanted, slow-growth forests within 200 miles of each workshop.

02

Shaping

CNC-guided rough cuts for consistency, then handed to a craftsperson for silhouette and detail work.

03

Sanding

Six passes by hand, from 80 to 220 grit, until every edge is soft enough for a toddler's grip.

04

Finishing

Water-based, food-safe dyes and a beeswax seal — no lacquer, no solvents, no synthetic gloss.

05

Assembly

Joints are glued and pinned by hand — never stapled — so every piece can be repaired, not replaced.

06

Safety Check

Drop-tested, choke-tested, and lab-certified before it ever reaches a box.

Materials We Trust

What actually
goes into a Stackly toy

No mystery plastics, no unnamed "other materials" on the label.

FSC-Certified Wood

Maple, beech, and cherry from forests independently certified for responsible replanting.

Water-Based Dyes

Pigments suspended in water, not solvent — safe if chewed, and gentle on the workshop air.

Organic Cotton & Wool

GOTS-certified cotton shells stuffed with untreated wool — no flame-retardant chemicals.

Plastic-Free Packaging

Recycled kraft board, soy ink, and a canvas tote instead of shrink wrap.
